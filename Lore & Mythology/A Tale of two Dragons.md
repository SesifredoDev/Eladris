## 📖 The Story

> 
> 
> 700 years ago, in the region that would later be known as Herwol. The Darakas tribe had been under the protection of the gracious wings of the Ancient Silver Dragon Syarthdarakasyn (See-arth-dar-acas-sin). The tribe had come under attack by an Ancient Red Dragon known as Tegom-Draggorath (Teh-gom Drag-gore-ath) due to perceived slights against him by the Darakas tribes expansion into territories he considered his alone.
> 
> Scores of Kobolds and dragonborns were slaughtered before Syarthdarakasyn and Tegom-Draggorath came to blows themselves. Syarthdarakasyn had long since given up any belief in reasoning with the red-scaled Darastrix (dragon). Their battle tore the skies and stained the ground for miles as they ripped each other apart by fang, claw, and powerful magics.
> 
> The battle lasted for days on end before silence permeated the area again. Eventually, both Syarthdarakasyn and Tegom-Draggorath were found. Still masses of slack, bloodied scales. It was clear that the red dragon had perished at the end before the tribe's Silver guardian succumbed to his own wounds.
> 
> Near the end of their fight, many heard Tegom-Draggorath shouts, vowing that he would return to destroy the tribe from within, to burn it in such great an inferno that it would be seen from the other side of the veil. Chilling the bones of the frost inclined dragonkin.
> 
> This threat eventually settled into story, and then to myth. An old tale to frighten children over trivial things. None knew the truth of his words.
> 
> In his pursuit of ever greater power, like many Dragons before and after him, Tegom-Draggorath had seeked the ability to live on after death, to return or remain in some way. This had led him into the clutches of Notch-Koron.  
> The deity had imparted knowledge of how to achieve a pale imitation of what the Gods themselves do, how to incarnate itself. To separate itself into parts that could eventually return to the Whole.
> 
> In a final bid to break their spirit before he died, or simply out of spite for the creature that had felled him, Tegom-Draggorath revealed this to Syarthdarakasyn.
> 
> He revealed that he would incarnate a part of himself into the Darakas Tribe, a festering flame that would burn it to the ground from within. Among the ashes of Syarthdarakasyn's precious tribe, the other part of Tegom-Draggorath would find it and reclaim his full strength. Intent to bring about a reign of terror upon the future he would find himself in.
> 
> Syarthdarakasyn, upon hearing this, knew he must do all he could to stem this great threat. Knowing that this menace could easily strike long after he was no longer able to protect the tribe himself he decided on a drastic measure. With his own claws he pierced his heart and with ancient words of power he bound his own essence to that of Tagom-Draggorath as the life faded from the Red Dragon.
> 
> Syarthdarakasyn tied himself to the part of Tagom-Draggorath that would eventually settle within his own tribe. With hope that he, in some way, could stem the corrupting influence of the Fire within the being.
> 
> As the dust settled, and time passed, almost 650 years later a Young Red dragon would find himself barraged with memories of a life he had not lived and a new name to go by, Tagom-Draggorath.
> 
> A few decades after the red dragon reawakened, a Dragonborn egg would be laid late but hatch early.
> 
> A boy with scales of silver and red blood.
> 
> Arjik Darakas
