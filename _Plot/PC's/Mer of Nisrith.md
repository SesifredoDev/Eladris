### Birth

The ocean lay still, a tranquil breeze running along its surface, casting subtle sprays into the air. It was at peace.

  

Below, deep below the ocean surface. Beyond the depths that any human diver could ever reach, beyond that even the greatest of the elves could accomplish. Deep enough that the pale blue of the ocean surface turned a somber navy. Yet in its somberness, centred atop a rolling hill across the sea bed lay a bustling city. Where once there was darkness, the merfolk of the ocean had built Niserith. It called to those around it, as if a lighthouse to those lost in the perils of the deep, golden light gleaming from the tips of its ivory towers.

  

Within the limits of the city, past the hustling crowds lay the religious quarter. Churches and temples to the divines line the streets as it was filled with pilgrims, priests and traders making their way past. Within one cathedral, its halls boomed with the chant of choirs, the sound echoing across the walls and up to the cavernous ceiling about. The line of pews filled with merfolk engaging in prayer and hymn, as upon a circular table in the centre of the cathedral lay two infant children.

  

As the hymn drew to a close, the head priest grabbed each one, swaddled in kelp, and raised them into the air.

  

Just as marrow gives us life, so too will they give life to the city and its citizens

So it is written

As they give life to this city, so too will they protect this city from harm

So it is written

The priest then lowered one, and raised the other higher

  

As bone gives the body strength, so too will he be strong, and grant our city strength

So he has entered

  

The priest raised the other into the air.

  

As glass shows what it clear and true, so too will he grant clarity and truth

So he has entered

  

The priest laid the two infants onto the table once more and the choir broke out into hymns.

  

Marrow-of-Bone and Marrow-of-Glass were born.

### Calamity

Marrow-of-Bone stood at attention. He had been standing there for the past three hours and by this point his joints began to hurt after spending so long forcing his body to stand still in a body of water. His breastplate chafed against his kelp tunic, its improper fit providing challenges all on its own.

  

“You know, when we became guards, Glass, I thought we’d be doing more than being tour guides”

  

His brother, Marrow of Glass, was opposite him. While they were twins, they hardly shared many similarities. Glass was tall where he was small. While he was the more outspoken of the two, Glass preferred to remain silent, casting quiet judgment over others. When he spoke, it was for a reason.

  

“I mean, I thought that there was gonna be a little more… excitement you know? Occasional thief, or something. I swear if someone asks where Strangeman’s jewelry store is I- I’ll do something that’s for sure.”

  

Glass, the living statue, turned towards him and nodded in agreement before turning to face forwards. This was most of their communication.

  

At that moment another guard ran up. Much to Bone’s embarrassment he couldn’t remember his name to save his life.

  

“Hey Bone, glad I caught you! Captain wants to see you in his office, not sure what its about though.”

  

“Really, me?”

  

“Yeah, strange I didn’t think he knew any of us by name. I’ll take over your shift till you get back.”

  

Bone gave his quick thanks before shuffling off towards the barracks.

  

In a city as ornate as Nesirith, with its tall ivory walls and gilded roofs, form became a function in of itself. Each entranceway was an ornate artisan craft, comprising months of hard labour carving exact shapes into giant clam shells to create interlocking doors. Years of careful growing and shaping of coral to create an entranceway to fit the doors. Where one could expect in a human city, a dull grey block of a barracks in its stead was a gilded hall, lined with table and wall decorations. Biolumenescent lamps cast light across the room, reflecting of the golden embellishments, tactically placed to brighten the room completely, leaving hardly a shadow on the dark ocean floor.

Bunks were still bunks, mind. Stack for quantity rather than quality, and one can only make sleeping with thirty others in a room so peaceful, but it was well known to each city watchman that those on the surface had it much worse. Bone couldn’t comprehend what worse could even be.

  

As he approached the captain’s office, the doors swung open as they sensed his presence. Sitting before him was the captain, a slightly heavier-set merfolk. Most of the time he could be found at his desk, scribbling away at some paperwork, making himself busy. It had been decades since he had been on patrol. It showed.

  

“Ah just the mer I wanted to see, take a seat, lad”

  

Bone sheepishly hovered himself over the stool.

  

“I called you in here as one of the lieutenants has since retired, you remember Shimmer of Joy don’t you?”

  

Bone recalled a rather stern man who would crack a whip if he saw even a spark of joy.

  

“Yes sir, he was a good instructor… and, er- passionate man”

  

“Quite, quite… anyways, I have been looking for a replacement for him amongst the staff, and looking at your track record, you’re a good worker, you seem to have a solid head on your shoulders. I want to offer you the position.”

  

Bone almost fell backwards.

  

“Wait, me sir? You want me?”

  

“Don’t see why not”

  

“I- I-” Bone stuttered “T-thank you sir I would graciously accept!” 

  

“Wonderful, you’ll find your new office on the corner. Equally, feel free to assign one of the other guards to help you attend to your duties. Any questions let me know.”

  

“Of course sir! Thank you so much!”

  

He regretted that as soon as he turned the corner and saw the mountain of paperwork.

### Volaris

Volaris felt queasy. It was ironic, out of all things he was queasy on a calm ocean.

  

But he had a good reason. His compatriots had been at sea for the past several days, exploring the Konkarha sea, searching the floor for any sign for their goal. Across this voyage they had been locked in mortal combat with the ocean as they made their way to their destination. Crashing waves and heavy storms had been daily occurrences, leaving him and his compatriots a healthy mixture of soaked, drenched, and generally soggy.  All until yesterday, where the storms and waves had stopped, leaving only tranquil waters. And it was not the sea as a whole, behind them, the storms still continued.

  

From his rudimentary instruments he had been able to map that the calmness existed in a perfect circle, outlining the boundary. The wind had changed direction too, drawing them further and further into the circle of calm.

  

They had arrived.

  

Volaris instinctively reached his hand to his waist, to his satchel. He fingered with the clasp before reaching in. His hands rested on his notes, his life’s work. It put him further at ease, but he was still on edge.

  

“Think this is it?”

### In the Absence of Hope

For a split second, the party beheld the majesty of space, as they beheld stars and the expanse of the universe that lay before them.

  

And yet it was only a fleeting memory, as just as soon as it arose, it had finished.

  

What was briefly cosmos yielded to a pale, blue sky and grass ground.

  

All except Bone who collapsed to his knees, gasping for air.

  

Volaris instinctively leapt back from the mer, whose hands once clasped at his lapels instead clutched his neck as he adjusted from the shock of switching from gill to lung. He felt the calm, the peace ebb away from him slowly, as suddenly the thousands of years within the city caught up to him at once, slamming into his psyche. Where there once was a sense of fulfilment, peace, tranquility, there was only hollow.

  

His hands slowly lifted from his neck to his eyes, and as he did so he saw the colour drain. The blueness of his skin bled from him, exiting from his fingertips like blood from an invisible, gaping wound in him. All that left was a pale, dour grey.

  

“I-it feels, so… empty” he was able to croak from his throat, that in the absence of the ocean ran dry and raspy.

  

At that, so too went the muscles in his back, as he began to faint.

  

Yet before he fell, Volaris dropped to one knee, his hand steadied Bone, as an uncorked potion met his lips.

  

“Steady, come on stay with me”

  

Volaris rifled in his satchel, full of parchment, ink and quills. His hand grasped at the scion before wrenching it out, gen still gleaming from the store of pure mana.

  

“Come on, work gods damn it, work”

  

Without another word he plunged the scion into the chest of the mer, as it cleaved through the ancient breastplate, now more rust than metal. The culmination of years of research and theory. As it made contact with bare carapace it glowed bright as the mana rushed in. Yet another gasp came from Bone as he felt emotion and feeling rush through his body once more. He fell forwards, his hands outstretched to catch his fall.

  

“It worked… it actually worked” Qing-jao said, her eyes disbelieving the very words she uttered. She had succeeded where no one had before. 

  

“A complete an unequivocal success of setting a shard! Haha HAAA” her graceful composure broken into a youthful laugh as she bunch her fists and raised them in triumph. It was met with silence and stares

  

“You call that a success…” Tesgan said, his voice barely a whisper. “We destroyed a city… committed a genocide against an innocent people, you call that a success!” His voice rose as the anger boiled over. “If that is what victory looks like for you Veilbinders then I’m not sure it worth the cost!”

  

Qing-jao, her momentary triumphant expression now replaced with a cold, unflinching stare. “We have done what no one thought could be possible, and set a festering Shard. Do you have even the slightest idea what this could mean? For us? For the world? We can finally reunite the continent, and if the price for the world is a city than I shall gladly pay it over again.”

  

Volaris rose from his knees.

“A reunited continent where one half is bathed in flame can hardly be called united.” 

  

As Qing-Jao turned to stare she found her gaze met by Volaris, his did not give. 

  

“I will ask you this once. Did you know what would happen?”

  

“Well the research was entirely theoretical in nature. There was no way one could know-“

  

“Yes or no. Did. You. Know?” His eyes filled with sorrow, remorse, and conflict.

  

Qing-jao sighed, “No. Each shard is different and full of unique mana energy. There was no way to know what would happen if the shard was set. That was the first shard setting in the century of the world.”

  

“Then I pray, for your sake, that this does not happen again” Tesgan snarled

  

At that Bone began coughing. Volaris took to one knee once more and clutched Bone’s shoulders with both hands. As he looked into the net’s face, just as soon as colour had started to return to the mer’s face, so too had the process started again.

  

It seemed inevitable.

  

A weak smile spread across Bone’s lips and he placed a hand on Volaris’ shoulder.

  

“Thank… you… for trying”

  

“Hey, stay with me. We can fix this. I’ve done research on it. It’s still in its early stages, there is a chance you can survive.”

  

“It is inevitable”

  

“We have treatments, we can slow down its progress.”

  

“So I can prolong my suffering? So I can watch myself slowly become a shell of the man I used to be?”

  

At that Volaris had no answer.

  

“No… I have seen what it has done to others. I’ve watched too many of them turn into husks. I’d prefer to go out on my own terms…”

  

“No!” Volaris yelled, his hands gripping tighter. “We freed you from the shard. You have the chance to live without being its slave.”

  

At that Bone chuckled softly. “It’s funny, you treat us as if we were prisoners, trapped by the stone. All we ever felt was calm, and safety. We lived our lives in tranquility, without fear. You think of us as slaves, yet all we wanted… was peace”

  

Volaris fell quiet

  

“You confuse quiet for peace. They are not the same”

  

Bone let out another, hacking cough as he felt numbness set into his joints and his bones, invading his marrow.

  

“Maybe so… but it was nice to believe”

  

“You were the servant of an artifact that distorted your life and made you thank it for the privilege. For giving you stagnation. You have freedom now, the ability to find a new reason to keep on going.”

  

Bokuve instinctively reached into his pack and pulled out a book, its cover weathered, worn and torn after years of use. He stepped over to the two and outstretched it. “This belonged to a good friend of ours, it helped him find purpose when he had none. Perhaps you’ll find what you need in there.”

  

He placed into Bone’s hands the book, and as he flicked open the front of the page, he saw the title:

  

Centius Glimmerstone’s A Guide to Positive Thinking: A Holistic Novel for finding Value and Self-Worth

  

His fingers traced the words across the page, his face riddled with conflict. Finally his expression is set, firm and determined.

  

“Marrow of Bone is dead” he said, matter-of-factly. 

  

He rose to one knee

  

“I am Marrow of Niserith, last of my kin, keeper of the spirit of my city. As my marrow grants me life, so too do I give my city life.”

  

He stood up, legs shaking with the effort.

  

“As long as I may live, so too does Niserith.”

  

And it was then, in the hills of Pebopetsia, that Marr was born.
